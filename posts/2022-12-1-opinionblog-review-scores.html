How I Review

How I Review

December 1, 2022

Blogs

Out of 5?

I review on a scale of 5, 1-5. I like that because it's simple and easy to understand. No half points, nothing to divide or question. 1-5.

But is that the best? Is 5 too limiting? Is a review of 5, the same as another review out of 5?

I originally chose the 1-5 review score from GoodReads.com, a book review site. I thought as I was reviewing the books that I read, or movies that I watched out of 5, so that I could write my reviews on GoodReads using their star system. I didn't want to do any math or change my score in anyway before posting my review, but do I need to change that?

What's a 4/5?

Is a 4/5 the same as an 8/10, or is it worse? My opinion, I think 8/10 is better than a 4/5. Because there is more granularity out of 10. But then why not go higher, 1-20? 1-50? 1-100?

With 1-100 you can get IN DEPTH, like for 1984 I could've given a 20/100, but at that point that's just a 1/5. So, I don't know..

And Now!

I'm trying a new way of reviewing, by implementing a new way to review - using a format taken from 2toRamble! And that’s the system!

Rate those 5 categories out of 10, average your score, and that’s your /10 Rambler Rating! You can rate each category to the nearest hundredth, tenth, whole number, or whatever you please.

My Review Scale

My Review Scale is as follows, and for more of a breakdown, click below to find out!

5/5: Extraordinary

This is reserved for things that I loved, and would without a doubt recommend. Something that deserves a 5/5 is something that I will tell everyone about - no matter the genre.

Example of 5/5

The Way of Kings by Brandon Sanderson, here is my review!

4/5: Great

This is reserved for things that almost made a 5/5, but I just felt it didn't make the mark.

Example of 4/5

The Hobbit by J.R.R. Tolkien, here is my review!

3/5: Okay

This is reserved for things that is really just okay - almost not even worth talking about. Not that the people that made it don't need any recognition, so that might be what I use the 3/5 for.. we'll see!

Example of 3/5

The Martian by Andy Weir, here is my review!

2/5: Bad

This is reserved for things that is BAD. Oh wow, something that gets a 2/5 infuriates me. I want to stop interacting with the item, but I have to tell all about it.

Example of 2/5

1984 by George Orwell, here is my review!

1/5: Terrible

This is reserved for something that is TERRIBLE. Literally terrible. Not something you should spend your time on - and I probably wasted my time reviewing it. But again, someone made this and everyone should hear about it.

I do not have an example of a 1/5 at this time.

So what's the best way to score?

As of right now, I'm going to stick with 1-5, but I'm going to try and be more stringent on my rating. We'll see!

I'm Going to Be More Harsh

Currently I don't have any 1/5, or 3/5. I have a mostly 5/5 score on everything that I've reviewed. I think I'll do better at determining a better score fr things. I'm also going to build a Hall of Extraordinary for everything 5/5!
