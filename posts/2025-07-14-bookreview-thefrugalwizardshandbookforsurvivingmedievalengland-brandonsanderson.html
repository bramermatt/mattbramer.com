A Misunderstood, Hilarious, Heartfelt Ride

Brandon Sanderson’s The Frugal Wizard’s Handbook for Surviving Medieval England isn’t the book most people expected it to be—and that’s exactly why I loved it. It’s strange, self-aware, and filled with a charm that feels like a breath of fresh air. Yes, the reviews have been mixed. And no, Frugal Wizard doesn’t follow the typical narrative structures of Stormlight Archive or the tight magic of Mistborn. But if you can approach this book on its own terms, what you’ll find is a delightfully oddball adventure that works because of its quirks, not in spite of them. I loved it!

Let’s address the elephant in the room: a lot of readers were disappointed. And in many ways, that disappointment isn’t about what the book is—it’s about what it isn’t. Fans expecting a deep, sprawling fantasy epic with lore-rich maps, hard magic systems, and epic stakes were left scratching their heads. This book doesn’t pretend to be that. It’s playful. It’s punchy. It pokes fun while casually jumping between dimensions and eras. It doesn’t build a detailed world so much as sketch it in with absurdity and background jokes. If you expected high fantasy, this might feel like a prank. But if you walk in ready to be entertained, it’s a blast. And I mean, wow, this was so much fun!

Embracing the Chaos

What I found so refreshing about Frugal Wizard was its sheer willingness to be different. The protagonist, who does experience amnesia, it still works. But I believe those who disagree with that, have been burned in the past before. The protagonist (who does have a name.. I won’t spoil for you) becomes a fascinating first-person to walk through this story with! He explores identity, redemption, and purpose. The “handbook” excerpts themselves are fun. clever, ridiculous, and oddly insightful. You really need to pay attention to them!

This book reminded me that Sanderson is more than just a builder of intricate worlds. He’s also a storyteller who knows how to have fun. Frugal Wizard feels like Sanderson giving himself permission to play, to experiment, and to write without the weight of world-ending stakes. And there’s something deeply enjoyable about watching a master of structure write something gleefully structure-less.

The Joy of Low-Stakes, High-Concept Fantasy

Not every book needs to change your life. Some books are meant to entertain you, surprise you, and sneak a little meaning in along the way. That’s what this book does. It’s funny and fast, but it also asks a few meaningful questions about who we are when everything familiar is stripped away. It’s not world-shattering, but it’s definitely worth the ride.

In some ways, I think Frugal Wizard will age better than many of Sanderson’s “mainstream” works. As expectations fade and readers start to encounter it on its own terms, I suspect it’ll find a second life as a favorite—beloved by those who weren’t afraid to enjoy it for what it is.

Final Thoughts

Is this book for everyone? Probably not. But if you’re tired of taking fantasy too seriously, or if you’ve ever wished your portal fantasy came with a satirical instruction manual, then this book is for you. I went in with an open mind, and I came out smiling.

Brandon Sanderson didn’t write another epic here. He wrote something rare: a book that makes fun of itself without losing its heart. And in a sea of overly serious fantasy, that feels like magic. Go Sanderson!
