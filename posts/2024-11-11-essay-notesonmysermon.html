Notes on My Sermon

November 11, 2024

Today has been a perplexing day. I preached a sermon yesterday and I was given some notes that have seemingly struck me to my core. Note 1: the sermon was directed at those who were already believers and followers of Jesus. Note 2: keep the sermons to 20-25 minutes as to keep the congregation engaged. I've also been feeling very "attacked' in my leadership, teaching, and my studying role.

On feeling attacked, I'm struggling to get a clear understanding of Scripture lately. It feels constant. Anytime I go into a passage I'm stymied. I get mixed up with the wording and lose the meaning of the passage. Maybe that is my translation (NASB2020). Or maybe that this is a spiritual battle that my mind is waging against me.

With the (NASB2020), I love it! I makes me feel good to read and know that this translation is very close to the original Hebrew and Greek. It is sometimes clunky to read, and that' just because it is the nature of translating those different languages to English. However, Scripture is God Breathed (2 Timothy 3:16). I do believe something is making it difficult for me to focus, and that could be any number of things.

Suffer For His Name | Acts 5:17-42 | Matthew Bramer | November 10, 2024

Note 1 struck me because it's true. My message was "Suffering for His Name" was directed at believers. I think I failed to draw in new believers with this message, though. This was a wake up call for me. I'll be better.

Hearing Note 2, I immediately got defensive. I worried about the congregations attention span, and I felt defensive of my preaching style, which involves a lot of questioning the congregation and leading them to be visible followers of Jesus. But, this is another wake up call for me. I've only recently started preaching and teaching more often, and I've been doing this for a solid year and a half now. This is something else I'll be working on.

P.S. I started reading (Young Patriots) today, and I'm very excited and interested to read this book. I've been studying the American Government and the Constitution for a couple weeks now. This is going to be a fun and insightful read, I hope! I'm revamping my website! I'm really working on the user-experience and user-interface. I'm hoping to make the site more usable and more accessible. I'm also starting a personal study in the book of Ecclesiastes. I need a new outlook on life, and I need some biblical wisdom for that.
