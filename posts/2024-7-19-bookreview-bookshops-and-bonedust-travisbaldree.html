Written Review

Well this was an interesting read! I heard all the hype regarding Badlree’s novel, Boookshops & Bonedust, and the hype certainly did not disappoint. Quick, witty, smart, and awesome. I love reading about the different races interacting. This is just fun and fantastical and it's awesome!

Every few pages I’m impressed at the prose, very elegant and intelligent. I’m learning so many new words!

This story seemed to keep me entertained, and without getting into my specific religious ideologies, the main relationship in the story was not too overpowering in the entirety of the book.

Overall, I feel this was a pretty good book. Luckily the prose and the magic system kept me engaged! I’m interested in reading more of Travis Baldree!

"Bookshops & Bonedust" by Travis Baldree is a delightful novel that blends the charm of bookshops with a touch of the fantastical. The story centers around a quaint bookshop in a small town that harbors more than just dusty old tomes. The protagonist, Viv’s career with the notorious mercenary company Rackam's Ravens isn't going as planned.

The characters add depth and diversity to the story, each with unique quirks and backgrounds. The interactions and relationships between the characters are a highlight of the book, providing both humor and heartfelt moments. Baldree's writing is engaging and fun, and certainly creates a cozy atmosphere of the bookshop and the enchanting world beyond its doors. The dialogue is witty and natural, adding to the overall charm of the narrative.

"Bookshops & Bonedust" explores themes of discovery, friendship, and the love of reading. The bookshop serves as a metaphor for the endless possibilities that books offer, inviting readers to explore new worlds and ideas. The story also emphasizes the importance of curiosity and courage in the face of the unknown, as well as the value of community and connection.
