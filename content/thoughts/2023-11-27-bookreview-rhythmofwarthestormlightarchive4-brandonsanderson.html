Rhythm of War (The Stormlight Archive, #4) Book Review

Rhythm of War (The Stormlight Archive, #4) by Brandon Sanderson

My rating: 5 of 5 stars

There is nothing better than a Brandon Sanderson book, four books deep in a series. LOVE IT. The Fused are everywhere. Dalinar is incredible. I love this story, I love this series. I love this book!

The world building has BLOWN UP this book. The magic system is matured and incredibly intelligent. Our characters are going through A LOT. This is the darkest we’ve ever seen some of our characters, yet there is so much redemption in this story. There is also terrible betrayals this book.

The Heralds are real! From the beginning of The Way of Kings, the Heralds have been these mythical figures that have been talked about and hinted at and now, they’re everywhere.

This is an incredible story, 1260 pages and I couldn’t be happier after finishing it. I’m constantly impressed by Brandon Sanderson and his storytelling. The Stormlight Archive, if it continues at this caliber of storytelling and worldbuilding (universe building), I can’t even fathom how this will end. Not just the in-world physics level, because this book goes deep on physics on an atomic level and it is breathtaking. Oh, it’s so good.

There are a lot of Cosmere Connections and Implications in this book. I’ve, at this point, only read Mistborn 1, Elantris, Warbreaker, Tress of the Emerald Sea, and now the entirety of the currently published Stormlight Archive and I can say… WOW. The Cosmere is real and it is highly implicated throughout this book. We are going off world soon!

I don’t want to spoil what makes this story great, but I will say I was surprised by some characters that I’d not particularly paid attention to before: Navani, Venli, Taravangian, Dabbid, Jasnah. These characters are smart, and I mean wicked smart, and they go deep emotionally and physically. I just love this book.

The flashback characters for this book are Venli and Eshoni. And whoa, does this book really make you care about these characters if you haven’t before. Whoa! I got answers to questions I didn’t even know I had while reading these flashback chapters.

Our main three, I’d call them - Kaladin, Shallan, Dalinar - are here and wow, they’re even more impressive this book. These characters, anytime they’re on the page, I am glued. I want to know everything about them, their history, their state of mind. Everything. They do not disappoint this book.

We get more Surges and more Orders of Knights Radiant acknowledged this book and holy crap, I love the Willshaper Order.

There is an incredible about of information given to us about the different Light in the world… that’s all I’ll say.

Navani, the Mother of Machines, good gracious does she go into so much detail about every little microscopic inner working of Stormlight and more this book. I COULD NOT GET ENOUGH. Brandon Sanderson, I feel, is a lot like myself - he finds something that is interesting and dives so deep into researching the topic that he becomes an expert in it. This book has music theory, physics, mathematics, cymatics, and so much more. My goodness. This is a HARD magic system with limits and the world is built with the sense that this all could possibly exist. Whoa!
