The Way of Edan (The Edan Trilogy, #1) Book Review

The Way of Edan (The Edan Trilogy, #1) by Philip Chase

My rating: 5 of 5 stars

Re-Read Review

Philip Chase's The Way of Edan is a book that's hard to put down. From the first page, you're drawn into a world of mystery, adventure, and magic that keeps you turning the pages long into the night.

To say more on this book would be fairly redundant, however, this is an excellent book. In all parts: world building, prose, characters, and magic. All supremely enjoyable and a want to read again and again!

Philip Chase has created a world that feels real and lived-in, with a rich history and culture that adds depth to the story. The prose is elegant and evocative, drawing you into the world of Edan with its vivid descriptions and lyrical language. The characters are well-drawn and complex, with their own motivations and desires that drive the plot forward. And the magic system is unique and intriguing, adding an extra layer of mystery and wonder to the story.

Overall, The Way of Edan is a captivating read that will keep you on the edge of your seat from start to finish. If you're a fan of epic fantasy with a touch of mystery and adventure, this is a book you won't want to miss.

Written Review
My, oh, my! What an outstanding novel by Dr. Philip Chase! Are YOU in the mood for a classic fantasy with the pacing of a modern fantasy story?! Are YOU interested in a self-published author? Are you interested in sorcerer / wizardry / medieval battle / gore / monsters?!

The Way of Edan
THE WAY OF EDAN IS IT FOR YOU! Oh my gosh, this book was absolutely electrifying. My goodness this story hooked me from the first page and didn't let go the entire time.

Classic Fantasy
I said "classic fantasy" and it is there, but this is a fresh take on a "chosen one" type fantasy story. And I was taken by the prose and world-building by this story; and this is book one in a trilogy. There is so much in this world that are beautiful and exciting and deep and I can't wait to read more.

World Building
This world is breathtaking and diverse and exciting. There is so much lore to this story and to this world, goodness I could gush on and on about this. You will not be disappointed reading this, and you will be helping out a self-published author and that's super cool.

Prose
I mentioned the prose, here is an example at which I fell in love the story: "Sequara's eyes widened a fraction as she released herself into the realm of origins... leaving behind her body, she joined her surroundings, aware of every emotion yet detached from all like an ethereal observer. She heard her voice chant the song of origin, "Druanil vardunay nanduinae...". This is what I want out of a fantasy story. I love the stories that have a slice of a world that is extra / multiple realm-type worlds.

Chosen One
This story is a chosen one story, that is familiar, but this is different. You might feel "ah this is just another chosen one story", but this is fresh. You'll see as you read.

Races
There are so much more than just humans here. There are humans, then DWARVES or Dweorgs, then Vikings or Thojns, DRAGONS, TROLLS, ELVES, these things called pucas that I find "golum" would be the best description. Absolutely incredible. These are all different type of races and peoples.

Violent
This is brutal and visceral. I'll just say that.

The Magic System
This is a super interesting "soft" magic system. This was very interconnected with the world and it's history and it's use. Sorcerers and Sorceresses, "songs" of origins, realm of the origins, realm of the forms. There is a sorceress who shoots fire from her fingertips. This is known as an empathic magic system, where people touch and reach toward minds and gain access to emotions. Emotions of everything: animals, beasts, humans, all.
