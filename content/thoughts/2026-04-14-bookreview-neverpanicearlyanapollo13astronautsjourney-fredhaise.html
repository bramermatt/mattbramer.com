Never Panic Early: An Apollo 13 Astronaut's Journey Book Review

Never Panic Early: An Apollo 13 Astronaut's Journey by Fred Haise

My rating: 4 of 5 stars

Ooooo this one was good.

Fred Haise brings a totally different feel than the other Apollo books I’ve been reading. It’s more personal, more technical, and you really feel that airman/test pilot mindset throughout.

This book is deep—but in a fast, gripping way. It doesn’t drag. It gets right into the systems, the training, the pressure, and what it actually takes to stay calm when everything is going wrong. And honestly, that’s what makes it so hard to put down.

I think the length really works in its favor too. It’s tight. The technical language never overstays its welcome, and it keeps the pace moving the whole time.

You’re not just reading about a mission—you’re stepping into the mindset of someone trained to solve problems under pressure.

Great read. Start to finish, it held me.
