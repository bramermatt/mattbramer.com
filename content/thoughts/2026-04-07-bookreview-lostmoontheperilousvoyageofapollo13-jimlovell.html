Lost Moon: The Perilous Voyage of Apollo 13 Book Review

Lost Moon: The Perilous Voyage of Apollo 13 by Jim Lovell

My rating: 4 of 5 stars

Alright—this one surprised me a little.

I went into Apollo 13 thinking, “I already know the story… something goes wrong, they fix it, everyone lives.” But the way it’s told—especially hearing it from Jim Lovell himself—makes it feel way more intense than I expected.

Once things start going wrong, it honestly feels like a slow-building crisis that just keeps getting worse. And even though you know the ending, you still catch yourself thinking, how in the world did they actually pull this off?

What I appreciated most is that it doesn’t just focus on the astronauts—it really shows how critical the people on the ground were. Mission Control comes across like a room full of people just refusing to let those guys die. It’s problem-solving at the highest level.

The audiobook version on Libby is really solid too. It’s easy to follow, not overly dramatic, but still carries the weight of what’s happening. It kind of feels like you’re listening in on history instead of just reading about it.

I will say—there are moments where it leans a little technical. Not enough to lose you, but enough where you might zone out for a second if you’re not locked in. But honestly, I think that adds to it—it reminds you this wasn’t luck, it was engineering and quick thinking.

At the end of the day, it’s not just a space story. It’s about pressure, teamwork, and staying calm when everything is going wrong.

I’d definitely recommend it—especially if you like space, problem-solving, or just a really good true story.
