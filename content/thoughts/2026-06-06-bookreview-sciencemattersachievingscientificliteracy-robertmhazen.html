Science Matters: Achieving Scientific Literacy Book Review

Science Matters: Achieving Scientific Literacy by Robert M. Hazen

My rating: 4 of 5 stars

I really enjoyed Science Matters. The authors do a great job taking topics that can feel intimidating and breaking them down into something understandable and interesting. As someone who is preparing to teach science, I found myself learning a lot and appreciating how the book explained concepts in a practical, approachable way.

In fact, for most of the book, I was already thinking about rereading it someday and even recommending it to some of my students. That's how much I was enjoying it.

My one disappointment came toward the end. The book shifted from explaining scientific concepts to making arguments for evolution and against creationism. Personally, I didn't care for that section. As a Christian who believes God created the world, I felt like the discussion became less about teaching science and more about challenging a viewpoint that I hold. Others may not feel the same way, but it definitely changed my experience of the final chapters.

That said, I don't want that criticism to overshadow what I liked about the book. The vast majority of Science Matters was engaging, informative, and very well written. I learned a lot from it, and I still think it does an excellent job introducing readers to many important scientific ideas. I just wish the ending had been a little more gracious toward people who hold different beliefs about origins.
