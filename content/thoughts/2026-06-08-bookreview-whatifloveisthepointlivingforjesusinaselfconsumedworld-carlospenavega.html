What If Love Is the Point?: Living for Jesus in a Self-Consumed World Book Review

What If Love Is the Point?: Living for Jesus in a Self-Consumed World by Carlos PenaVega

My rating: 5 of 5 stars

I really enjoyed What If Love Is the Point? by Alexa and Carlos PenaVega.

What I appreciated most was that this wasn’t just a book about marriage or relationships—it was a book about the gospel. Throughout their story, they continually point back to Jesus and the way He has worked in their lives. It was encouraging to read about God’s grace, redemption, and faithfulness through the experiences of two people who happen to be well-known but face many of the same struggles and questions we all do.

As the title suggests, love really is the point of this book. There are some great insights on marriage, a few helpful thoughts on parenting, and practical encouragement for everyday Christian living. One section that especially stood out to me was their discussion about praying for “EGR” (Extra Grace Required) people—the people who can be difficult to love. That was both convicting and helpful.

The book is an easy, quick read, but it still gives you plenty to think about. Whether you’re married, dating, raising kids, or simply trying to follow Christ more faithfully, there’s something here worth taking away.

Overall, I found it encouraging, Christ-centered, and uplifting. I’d definitely recommend it.
