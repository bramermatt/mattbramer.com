Knife of Dreams (The Wheel of Time, #11) Book Review

Knife of Dreams (The Wheel of Time, #11) by Robert Jordan

My rating: 5 of 5 stars

Written Review

Robert Jordan, an absolutely beautiful installment in the Wheel of Time and your final Wheel of Time book prior to your untimely passing. This was a spectacular book, specifically in the series, but also a book. There is action, intrigue, resolutions, spontaneity, and so much more happening. After multiple books of slow progress we get a book of resolutions! This was just amazing! More to come in the spoiler review.

Spoiler Review

Oh what a victory lap from Robert Jordan with this book. An absolutly amazing book in this series. SO MANY RESOLUTIONS IN THIS BOOK, let's go through it all! And, most of this will be more from the Story Breakdown...

Thom reveals to Mat that Moiraine is alive and being held captive by the Aelfinn and Eelfinn. They formulate a plan to infiltrate the Aelfinn and Eelfin using the Tower of Ghenjei thanks to Olver’s knowledge of the game of Snakes and Foxes. Mat and Noal agree to accompany Thom on his mission to save Moiraine. Perrin finally saves Faile.. FINALLY! She has been taken for three books now, and wow, finally we got the saving. Elayne is now Queen of Andor! Mat reunites with Talmanes and an even larger and better equipped Band of the Red Hand. Together they launch a campaign of guerilla warfare against the Seanchan army sent by Suroth to murder Tuon. They are found by Furyk Karede, who takes over protection of Tuon and gives Mat intelligence to destroy the Seanchan force. Before returning and assuming power in Ebou Dar, Tuon completes the marriage ceremony with Mat, making him the Prince of Ravens. Rand goes to meet with the Daughter of the Nine Moons, though he discovers it is Semirhage in disguise. Rand loses his hand in the confrontation, burned away by the Forsaken’s attack while Lews Therin battled him for control of saidin. Semirhage is taken captive. Both the rebel Aes Sedai and Red Sisters from the Tower send representatives to the Black Tower to bond Asha’man as Warders. Taim agrees and says, “Let the Lord of Chaos rule,” a phrase previously used by the Forsaken. WHAT COULD THAT MEAN?!
