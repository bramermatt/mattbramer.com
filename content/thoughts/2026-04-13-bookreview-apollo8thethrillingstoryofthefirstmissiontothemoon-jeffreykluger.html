Apollo 8: The Thrilling Story of the First Mission to the Moon Book Review

Apollo 8: The Thrilling Story of the First Mission to the Moon by Jeffrey Kluger

My rating: 5 of 5 stars

I just finished Apollo 8, and honestly… this was just awesome.

I’ve been kind of hyper-fixated on space lately, and this book hit exactly what I was looking for. It has that same feel as Apollo 13—real people, real pressure, and incredible problem-solving—but this one leans more into the wonder of it all.

What stood out to me most is how big this mission really was. These guys were the first humans to leave Earth’s orbit and go to the moon—like… actually stop and think about that. The science behind it is fascinating, but it never feels overwhelming. It actually makes it more exciting because you realize how much had to go right.

And then there’s the human side. These weren’t just astronauts—they were husbands, fathers, guys with personalities and fears. The book does a great job showing both the courage and the weight of what they were doing.

The “Earthrise” moment and the Christmas Eve broadcast were probably my favorite parts. There’s something about that scene that just hits deeper than science—it feels meaningful in a bigger way.

If you enjoy space, history, or just stories about people doing something incredibly difficult and doing it well, I’d definitely recommend this.

This one just made me love space even more.
