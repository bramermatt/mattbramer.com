A Memory of Light (The Wheel of Time #14) Book Review

A Memory of Light (The Wheel of Time #14) by Robert Jordan

My rating: 5 of 5 stars

Written Review


This has been one of the most impactful books I've ever read. Not just the Wheel of Time, but in the fantasy genre. This is an incredible ending to the Wheel of Time. I couldn't have asked for a better ending. You will be TIRED after reading this book. You will have to grab tissues, and you'll have to make sure you have some hydration as you read. Why? Because when you start reading this book, you won't be able to stop.

This book is action-PACKED! I'd say this book is 95% action. You are on such a high for 95% of this book, the last 5% you have to hold on TIGHT. You might struggle at points, but you ARE IN FOR A TREAT!

I think, and I've said it before, Brandon Sanderson was perfect to complete this series. Now, Brandon has said that Robert Jordan had written the ending to these books YEARS ago. Brandon has also said that RJ had written the epilogue with very little touched by Brandon.

Since Brandon has taken over, the magic system has been more deeply integrated - if that was even possible. Robert Jordan, in his passing, left Brandon such a well developed story - in my eyes. Robert Jordan is a master in story telling. Brandon Sanderson is a master in story telling.

Also, I love Perrin now. He has become one of my favorite characters since The Gathering Storm. If only he had been this incredible in books 5-12.

I can't believe I finished The Wheel of Time... wow.

Spoiler Story Breakdown


To do this book justice.. this is going to be difficult.

This book starts on a bang, Trollocs invading Caemlyn from the ending of TOM. And here begins our Trollocs battle. And that won't stop until page 970. Does that sound like a lot? YES. BUT! YOU WILL NOT BE TIRED OF THEM BY THEN. Holy smokes. I was struggling at the beginning of this book because of the Trollocs, because they were EVERYWHERE. You'd go over here and they were there, you'd go over there and they were there.

But we are DEEP in the Last Battle. And GOOD GRACIOUS! The Last Battle in this book is 216 pages, one chapter. An INCREDIBLY EPIC BATTLE, like hard to describe actually. But there are over 50 perspectives.. 50 perspectives. But I'm jumping ahead of myself.

A couple standout characters here that surprised me.. Perrin, Androl, Olver, and Birgitte. Perrin has come up a lot in the last books, as I've said, and he is just AWESOME in this book!

Let's dive in.
Thank you to {Book Summary: A Memory of Light - Dragonmount} for the helpful summaries here.

The war begins on four fronts. Elayne and Bashere lead Andorans against the Trolloc army in Caemlyn. Agelmar and the Borderlander armies support Lan’s forces at Tarwin’s Gap. Gareth Bryne and Egwene face an army in Kandor. Ituralde and the Aiel will lead the forces at Shayol Ghul in defense of Rand. Faile is placed in charge of supplies and entrusted with the Horn of Valere.

At the Black Tower, Androl and Pevara bond one another and lead the infiltration in the Black Tower to save Logain, eventually driving out Taim and the Dreadlords that were being Turned to the Shadow.

Perrin and Gaul have Rand help them travel to Tel’aran’rhiod in the flesh to hunt Slayer. Rand arrives at Shayol Ghul and enters the cave. Perrin and the wolves save him from Slayer. Perrin also discovers Compulsion is being used to manipulate the four great captains commanding the forces of the Light. Egwene and the Aes Sedai army in Kandor are overrun when Demandred arrives with all the forces of Shara. They are eventually reinforced by the arrival of the Seanchan. Similarly, Bashere leads Elayne’s army into a trap outside Cairhien, but they are saved by Logain, Androl, and the Black Tower.

Mat takes control of all the armies of the Light and prepares a final stand at the Fields of Merrilor. But Faile, Olver, and the Horn are lost in the Blight.

Rand duels Moridin, then faces the Dark One with conflicting possible futures outside of the Pattern while the Final Battle rages. During the prolonged battle, Demandred duels Gawyn and kills him, then wounds Galad, while dueling Mat’s army.

Losses in battle also include Rhuarc, Siuan, Bryne, Birgitte, and more. Mat sends the Seanchan away in a feigned retreat. Egwene defeats Taim and the Dreadlords with a new weave, the Flame of Tar Valon, which saves the Pattern from the damage of balefire. Lan duels Demandred and finally kills the Forsaken commander. At the same time, the return of the Seanchan and Olver blowing the Horn of Valere turns the tide and they defeat the army of the Shadow and the Sharans.

Logain breaks the seals and Rand baits Moridin into using Callandor. Rand then uses saidin, saidar, and the True Power to build a new, perfect prison for the Dark One. In the final stage of battle, Perrin protect Rand by killing Slayer and Cyndane. Then Mat arrives and destroys Padan Fain.

Rand is believed to have died of his wounds after the battle and a funeral pyre is lit. Aviendha, Elayne, and Min know he survived in Moridin’s body as he rides away in secret, unable to channel but still affecting the Pattern directly.

My Thoughts


Rand
This was an incredibly flawless ending to this series. Rand going off in the sunset, being able to hide. Rand doing this body-swap with Moridin, because of the balefire crossing in POD, I have been thinking about the balefires crossing since then and it comes to fruition here. Rand also lighting the pipe.. I agree with Brandon Sanderson’s thoughts, that Rand being so intwined with the Pattern - he can just manifest things now. How powerful!

Egwene
I’d been spoiled on Egwene’s death a long time ago. However, her death scene was still so impactful. From TFOH, I’d fallen in love with Egwene. And from TGS she has become just incredible and every scene she’s on the page in itching for more. So her death, while sacrificing herself was perfect.

Nynaeve
To think about Nynaeve, specifically in AMOL, is difficult. But also the question is brought up, what did Moiraine do here besides be there? Nynaeve, we’ve heard is the most powerful female channeler that has been for over 1,000 years. But in this book, she stood next to Rand in Shayol Ghul, then Linked with Rand. Then gave Alanna some herbs as a great Wisdom would do. But what else? Nynaeve was one of my favorites for a VERY LONG TIME! I’m not upset, there was so much more that occurred in this book. Just a thought.

Mat
Son of Battles! Oh man Mat, this was INCREDIBLE! I loved everything Mat here.

Perrin
Oh Perrin, I’ve gushed over you this review and I can’t stop. YOU ARE SO POWERFUL and so AWESOME! I apologize that it took me this long to realize. You’re amazing. TAR is yours. Also, I’m really glad Faile was alive.

Olver
Olver blew the Horn of Valere! That is awesome!

Gawyn
People have blown Gawyn out of proportion, in my humble opinion. He put on the bloodknife rings and ran to kill Damandred. He got himself killed and Egwene was shaken to her core, but that didn't really do much. This wasn't too big of a deal to me.

The New Seal on the Dark One's Prison
Logain breaks the seals and Rand baits Moridin into using Callandor. Rand then uses saidin, saidar, and the True Power to build a new, perfect prison for the Dark One. This was awesome. For the whole battle with Rand and the DO throughout the entirety of this book, I was impressed with this realization. Does this mean the DO is sealed forever.. I think so!
