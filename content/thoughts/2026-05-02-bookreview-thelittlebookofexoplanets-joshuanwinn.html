The Little Book of Exoplanets Book Review

The Little Book of Exoplanets by Joshua N. Winn

My rating: 5 of 5 stars

Joshua Winn’s The Little Book of Exoplanets sits just a bit beyond my current scientific comfort zone—but in the best way possible. At times it stretched me, even went slightly over my head, yet it never lost my attention. Winn has a way of making complex ideas feel inviting rather than intimidating.

Each chapter left me thinking more deeply about planetary science, astrophysics, general physics, and even the broader world of maths. It’s the kind of book that doesn’t just inform—it sparks curiosity. I found myself wanting to keep learning long after I put it down.

Challenging, engaging, and surprisingly fun—this is a great read for anyone willing to stretch their mind just a little further into the universe.
